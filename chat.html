A swap means you and the other learner exchange your existing tests. By law you each call DVSA yourselves — no one can do it for you, and it must be done by phone (not online, email, text or WhatsApp). Work through the steps below together.

Step 1 — Swap booking references

⚠️ Share ONLY your booking reference here. NEVER share your driving licence number, payment card, address, phone number or email with the other learner. Give those to DVSA only.

The other learner's booking reference — read this to DVSA
You will not be asked for their name or contact details — only this reference number.

Step 2 — Confirm your test details

Enter the test you have booked now. Once you confirm it's correct, you'll both see the slot you're swapping into, and the eligibility checks will appear.

The slot you would swap into
This is the other learner's current test. Check it works for you and your instructor.

Step 3 — Check you both qualify

Confirm your test details in Step 2 first — the checks will appear here.

Both of you must be able to tick all of these. If either of you can't, the swap can't go ahead.

Step 4 — Have these in front of you before you call

  • Your own driving licence number
  • Your own booking reference number
  • The payment card you booked with (DVSA will never ask for the full number or security code)
  • Details of any driving tests you've taken before
  • The other learner's booking reference (shown in Step 1)

Step 5 — Call DVSA, both online at once

You both need to be at your phones at the same time. One of you calls; DVSA puts that person on hold and rings the other learner on the number saved on their booking. Tap below only when you're genuinely ready to call right now.

✅ You're both ready — make the call now
DVSA: 0300 200 1122 — choose option 1
Monday to Friday, 8am to 5pm
Decide between you who calls.
  1. The caller phones DVSA, passes the security checks, and confirms they agree to the swap.
  2. The caller gives DVSA the other learner's booking reference (shown in Step 1 above).
  3. DVSA puts the caller on hold and phones the other learner on the number saved on their booking.
  4. The other learner passes security checks and confirms they agree to the swap.
  5. DVSA completes the swap. Write down your new test details — you won't get an email, but your next DVSA reminder will show the new slot.

DVSA won't complete the swap if either of you fails security, doesn't agree, or has used both changes — or if the other learner doesn't answer, or the phone number on their booking is wrong. Check your booking has your correct phone number before you call.
